# fastners

## Overview
fasteners (aka screws)

Every fastener is in metric. With the exception of HDD fasteners.

Every fastener **MUST BE** magnetic.
- NOTE: Not all stainless steel is magnetic

Prefer "pan washer/wafer head" or "flanged button head" fasteners for the motherboard.

Generally, do not use M3 fasteners to fasten anything that requires strength, M5 or M6 seems to be the standard, below is a chart of nominal stress area (higher is stronger):
- M3 0.5: 5.03
- M3 3.5: 6.78
- M4 0.7: 8.78
- M5 0.8: 14.2
- M6 1.0: 20.1

The weakest points in a bolt is the stressed area in the thread root. The stressed area depends on the type of threads - coarse threads reduces the stressed area compared to fine threads. This is the nominal stress area.

### Motherboard/case fasteners
Cases are tapped to M3 0.5

M3 0.5 3mm, 6mm

M3 0.5 3mm are used for joining case parts

M3 0.5 6mm are used for joining motherboard to standoffs

### Motherboard/case standoffs
Motherboard standoffs are M3 0.5 10mm so that wires can run under if needed

Standoffs prevent the motherboard from shorting on the case

### HDD fasteners
6-32 3/16", unfortunately these are not metric, this is the western digital specification

### Rack fasteners
Rack fasteners should use M5 0.8 10mm fasteners
